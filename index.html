A verified employee referral marketplace for India

Referrals make up only 7% of all applicants to a company. But they produce 30–50% of all successful hires. In India, this runs on LinkedIn DMs and WhatsApp groups.

The Concept

Three users, one transaction

A job seeker opens the app and swipes through verified Google employees who are open to referring in the engineering domain. Each referrer has a trust score, response SLA, and hire success rate. The seeker swipes right on a match, sends their profile, and the referrer — if the profile fits — submits them through Google's internal system.

On a successful hire, Google pays the referrer a ₹50,000 bonus (existing company policy). The platform takes 15–20% (₹10K) and charges Google a ₹12K success fee. The referrer earns ₹40K they never would have otherwise. The seeker gets a job they never would have gotten through Naukri.

1. Discover — Swipe Stack

The primary screen for job seekers. Referrer profiles presented as a card stack (Tinder mechanic). Cards stack with depth — next card visible behind at smaller scale. Three actions: skip (✕), request referral (✓), view full profile (→).

Card information hierarchy: Company + city at top (the #1 filter seekers care about), name and role, skill chips, then trust metrics. Everything needed to make a swipe decision in 3 seconds.

XP / Level pill: Gamification visible from the first screen. Seekers earn XP for completing their profile, getting referred, getting hired. Referrers earn XP for responding fast, accepting requests, successful hires.

Swipe right = variable reward: "Will this referrer accept me?" is the dopamine loop. Same psychology as dating apps — uncertainty of acceptance drives engagement.

2. Swipe Right → "REFER" Stamp

When the seeker drags the card right past the threshold, a green "REFER" stamp appears on the card. On release, the card flies off-screen and the seeker is prompted to confirm the request (optional note, resume attached automatically).

The stamp is the commitment moment. Seeing "REFER" appear on the card creates a physical sense of action — you're doing something, not just browsing. This is the Tinder "It's a Match" equivalent.

Swipe left = "SKIP" stamp (red, card flies left). No penalty. The referrer never knows they were skipped. Seeker can always go back via search.

Anti-spam limit: Free tier: 3 referral requests/month. Premium: unlimited. Visible counter prevents spray-and-pray behavior.

3. Referrer Profile — Full View

Accessed by tapping the → button or tapping a card. This is where the seeker evaluates if this referrer is worth their limited request quota.

Trust score is not a single number. Broken into response time, accept rate, and hires — so the seeker can distinguish a fast-but-picky referrer from a slow-but-generous one.

Domain skill bars: Shows what this referrer can credibly refer for. Aditya is 95% Backend but only 55% DevOps — don't ask him for an SRE referral.

Candidate reviews (the moat): Real feedback from people who were referred by this person. Review data compounds over time and can't be replicated by a new entrant.

9/9 verification badge: Corporate email ✓, LinkedIn match ✓, tenure >6mo ✓, domain relevance ✓, historical audit ✓, candidate feedback ✓, SLA tracking ✓, employment letter ✓, re-verification ✓.

4. Referrer Inbox — Review Requests

This is the referrer's primary screen. Incoming referral requests, pre-filtered by the platform's AI. The referrer should be able to evaluate and decide in under 60 seconds per request.

Match % is the first thing you see. 88% match = this candidate's skills closely align with open roles at your company. 64% = weaker match, maybe skip. The referrer doesn't need to read the resume — the platform already did.

"Accept · ₹40K–80K" — earnings potential is on the button itself. The referrer knows exactly what they'll earn. This is intentional — it reminds them this isn't charity, it's compensated work.

48hr SLA countdown. If a referrer doesn't respond in 48 hours, the request auto-expires and their response rate drops — which lowers their trust score and card placement. Creates urgency without being pushy.

On Accept: Referrer sees instructions for submitting through their company's internal ATS. Platform tracks the submission status. On Decline: brief reason selection (helps the AI learn). Candidate is notified without seeing the reason.

5. Earnings + Gamification

The referrer retention engine. This screen makes earning from referrals feel like progress in a game, not a one-off transaction.

Earnings hero card: Total earned prominently displayed. Referrers see the cumulative value of their participation — ₹1.24L from 12 hires. This is the "portfolio value" feeling.

Daily streak (Duolingo mechanic): Respond to at least one request per day to maintain streak. 7-day streak = ₹500 bonus. Streak breaks = restart. Creates daily habit of checking the app.

Level system (Bumble-inspired): Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum. Each level unlocks: higher card placement (more seekers see you), priority support, and higher take-rate (referrer keeps 85% at Platinum vs 80% at Bronze).

Transaction feed: Every payout, bonus, and XP event logged. Transparency builds trust. The referrer can see exactly when and why money was earned.

Roadmap

Everything needed to start this from scratch

1
Company Formation & Infrastructure
  • LegalRegister company (Pvt Ltd or LLP). GST registration. DPIIT startup recognition. Draft co-founder agreement with equity vesting.
  • LegalTerms of service, privacy policy, referrer agreement (IP, liability, payout terms). Consult employment lawyer on referral bonus taxation (TDS implications for referrer payouts).
  • BankingOpen business current account. Setup Razorpay/Cashfree for payment processing. UPI integration for referrer payouts. Escrow arrangement for employer success fees.
  • InfraCloud setup — AWS India (Mumbai region) or GCP. PostgreSQL on RDS/Cloud SQL. Redis for caching. S3 for resume storage. CDN for static assets.
  • InfraCI/CD pipeline (GitHub Actions or GitLab CI). Staging + production environments. Monitoring (Grafana/Datadog). Error tracking (Sentry). Logging (ELK or CloudWatch).
  • InfraAuth system — OTP-based login (Indian standard), Google OAuth, LinkedIn OAuth. Email service (SES/Postmark). SMS gateway (Twilio/MSG91 for Indian numbers).
  • DomainRegister domain. Setup DNS, SSL, email (workspace). Create brand identity (logo, colors, typography). Social accounts (LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram).
  • TeamFounding team roles: product + engineering (can be same person), design (can be contracted), and one person focused entirely on supply-side BD (recruiting referrers).
2
MVP Build — Prove the Referral Loop
  • ProductSeeker onboarding: phone/email signup → profile creation → resume upload (PDF parse to extract skills) → target company selection.
  • ProductReferrer onboarding: LinkedIn OAuth → corporate email verification (OTP to @company.com) → profile setup (domain expertise, availability toggle).
  • ProductCompany directory: seeded database of top 20 companies in Bangalore with open role data (scraped from career pages or manual entry).
  • ProductSwipe card stack (Discover screen): referrer cards with trust metrics, skill chips, company badge. Swipe right = request, left = skip.
  • ProductReferrer profile view: trust score breakdown, domain skill bars, candidate reviews (initially empty — seed with onboarding survey data).
  • ProductReferral request flow: seeker sends request → referrer receives in Inbox → accept/decline → on accept, show "Submit through [Company] internal system" instructions.
  • ProductStatus tracking pipeline: Request → Accepted → Submitted → Interviewing → Offered → Hired. Both parties see real-time status updates via push notifications.
  • ProductReferrer inbox with match % (basic keyword matching v1 — not AI yet), skill chips, accept/decline buttons, earnings estimate, SLA timer.
  • OpsManual payout process: on confirmed hire, verify via referrer screenshot of internal ATS → trigger UPI payout minus platform commission. Manual is fine at <50 hires.
  • GrowthSeed supply: recruit 100 referrers at 20 Bangalore companies. Source from LinkedIn #OpenToRefer users, Fishbowl IT Job Referral India bowl. Offer founding badge + zero take rate initially.
  • GrowthSeed demand: SEO content ("how to get referral at Google India"), LinkedIn content from verified referrers, Product Hunt India launch.
  • MetricNorth star for this phase: 10 complete referral→hire→payout loops. Everything else is noise until this works.
3
Trust Engine, AI Matching & Gamification
  • ProductAI match scoring: train on successful referral data. Input = seeker skills/experience, output = match % against role requirements. Replace keyword matching with embeddings-based similarity.
  • ProductResume gap analysis: compare seeker profile to role requirements, surface specific gaps ("Add Kubernetes experience") with actionable suggestions.
  • ProductDynamic trust scores: composite of response time (SLA adherence), acceptance rate, hire conversion, candidate feedback, recency. Recalculated daily.
  • ProductCandidate review system: after every referral interaction (accept, decline, or hire), candidate rates the referrer. 5-star + free text. Displayed on profile.
  • ProductGamification layer: XP system, level progression (Bronze→Silver→Gold→Platinum), daily response streaks with streak bonuses (₹500 for 7-day), level-up rewards (higher take rate, priority placement).
  • ProductEarnings wallet: in-app wallet showing balance, pending payouts, transaction history. Withdrawal to UPI/bank. Automated payout on hire confirmation.
  • ProductAnti-spam pre-filtering: before a request reaches the referrer, AI checks if the seeker's profile meets minimum match threshold. Rejects mismatches with feedback to seeker.
  • ProductPremium seeker tier: ₹499–₹1,999/month. Unlimited referral requests, AI resume optimization, priority in referrer queues, skill gap analysis. Free tier: 3 requests/month.
  • GrowthExpand to Hyderabad (Microsoft, Amazon, Google GDC, ServiceNow) and Pune (Persistent, Barclays, Cummins). Same playbook: seed supply first.
  • MetricNorth star: 500 referrers, 10K seekers, 30% monthly referrer retention, 50+ hires/month.
4
Employer SaaS — The Revenue Engine
  • ProductEmployer dashboard: branded referral portal showing all referrals flowing into the company, conversion funnel, time-to-fill metrics, top referrer leaderboard.
  • ProductATS integration: Zoho Recruit, Darwinbox, Keka, Greenhouse, Lever. Two-way sync — referral submitted on platform → auto-creates candidate in company's ATS.
  • ProductReferral analytics: which departments get the most referrals, which referrers are most effective, cost-per-hire comparison vs other channels, retention tracking.
  • ProductEmployee engagement tools: internal leaderboards (gamify referrals within the company), referral campaigns ("We're hiring 10 engineers this quarter — bonus doubled"), Slack/Teams integration.
  • ProductCompliance module: referral bonus policy management, TDS certificate generation for referrer payouts, audit trail for all referral transactions.
  • SalesEnterprise sales: target HR heads at 50+ companies with transaction data ("We've already placed 15 engineers at your company through our platform — here's the data"). Pricing: ₹15K–50K/month.
  • GrowthExpand to Mumbai + Delhi-NCR. Target: 5 cities, 50K+ referrers, 500+ employer accounts.
  • OpsAutomated payout infrastructure: bulk UPI payouts, TDS deduction and filing, referrer PAN collection and verification, GST invoicing for employer fees.
  • MetricNorth star: ₹15Cr+ ARR from SaaS. This is the real business.
